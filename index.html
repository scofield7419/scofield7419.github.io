Profile

I am currently a postdoc research fellow in NExT++ lab, School of Computing, National University of Singapore, supervised by Prof. Tat-Seng Chua. Before that, I received my Ph.D degree at Wuhan University.
My research direction is natural language processing (NLP), with the fundamental goal of building models capable of human-level understanding of natural language. My interests have revolved around syntactic structure-aware NLP, specifically, integrating syntax/semantic structure as external knowledge for improving downstream tasks. I am apt to construct models with deep learning technique, especially with the language structure. My research works have been published in top-tier NLP/DM/ML venues, e.g., ICML, ACL, AAAI, WWW, SIGIR, IJCAI, EMNLP, COLING, TOIS, TNNLS, TASLP, etc. I also work closely with these NLP scholars, Meishan Zhang, Chenliang Li, Fei Li, Yafeng Ren, Yue Zhang, etc.

My ongoing research directions continue the structure-aware spirit but with broader scenario, i.e., integrating syntax/semantic structure into such as information extraction, affective computing, multimodal, language models, text generation, etc., which specifically include:

  • Information Extraction, e.g., complex NER, relation extraction, event extraction.
  • Knowledge-driven Langauge Models, e.g., enhancing PLMs with knowledge from KG, language grammars, logics.
  • Multimodality, e.g., with the aid of structure information.
  • Affective Computing, e.g., structured sentiment analysis, opinion pair/triplet/quadruple extraction.
  • Language Structure Parsing, e.g., syntax parsing, semantic parsing and universal semantic graph parsing.
